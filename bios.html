Bios

Student and contributor bios.

Allison Hinton
Allison Hinton is a student in English 1102 at Georgia Tech, pursuing a bachelor's degree in Aerospace Engineering. For this project, her work focuses on examining social inequality and the role of education in shaping opportunity and identity, both in literature and in real-world contexts. She contributed the section analyzing the lowest-class clones in Never Let Me Go, exploring how lack of access to education predetermines life outcomes and reinforces systemic inequality.
Rinburche "Rin" Duong
Rinburche "Rin" Duong is a student at Georgia Tech currently taking English 1102. He is pursuing a bachelor's degree in industrial design. Within this project concerning the film Never Let Me Go, he and his team discuss how the rigid hierarchy within the world of the narrative reflects modern day social mobility in relation to education access and inequality. He contributed to the construction of the analysis section of the "About Me" section and an analysis of the "normal" individuals in the film and their representation of the upper class.
Grant Eastwood
Grant Eastwood is a student in English 1102 at Georgia Tech and a current second-year Computer Science major. For this project, his main work was development of the website showcasing the team's research and analyzing the middle social class connection to the movie Never Let Me Go.
